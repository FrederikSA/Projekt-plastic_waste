Where do ocean plastics end up?

Facts about where ocean plastics end up

Around 1.7 million tonnes of plastic enter the oceans each year. This diagram shows where the plastic comes from and where it ends up.

The majority of ocean plastic (82%) comes from rivers, while 18% comes from coastal areas.

Once in the ocean, most of the plastic (88%) remains floating near the shoreline, 10% sinks to the seabed, and just 2% is transported offshore.

Global Plastic Production Over Time

Facts about plastic production

More than 350 million tonnes of plastic waste are produced globally each year since 2013.

Over 40% of produced plastic is single-use plastic, used for less than a year.

19 million tonnes of plastic is leaked to the environment. 13 million tonnes to terrestrial environments, and 6 million tonnes to rivers or coastlines.

Plastic comparison by water

Facts about plastic waste in the oceans

The world's oceans cover about 71% of Earth's surface and contain 97% of all the water on the planet.

It is estimated that around 1.7 million tonnes of plastic waste enter the oceans every year.

This means there is approximately 15 grams of plastic per cubic meter of ocean water.
